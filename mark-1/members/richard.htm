James E. Richard Sr.

Wednesday, Sep. 14, 2005
©The Virginian-Pilot

VIRGINIA BEACH -- Retired U.S. Navy Cmdr. James E. Richard Sr. passed away Saturday, Sept. 10, 2005.

He was born in Taft, Calif., Oct. 16, 1924. He served 30 years in the U.S. Navy earning a Silver Star in World War II while aboard the USS Barb (SS-220), one of the most decorated submarines in U.S. history. After retiring from the Navy in 1974, he worked for the defense contractor QED Systems until 1995. He was a life member of the Virginia Beach Volunteer Rescue Squad, serving 25 years in numerous positions.

He is survived by his beloved wife of 59 years, Helen Slentz Richard; their five children, Yvette Dyson and her husband David, Bernadette Magne and her husband Tom, James E. Richard Jr. and his wife Judy, Tim Richard and his wife Susan and Suzette Caddell and her husband Brian; their grandchildren, Rebecca Dyson Allen and her husband Jerry, Jeffrey Dyson and his wife Jennifer, Jason Dyson, Bridgette Magne, Michael Magne and his wife Shannon, David Hogue, Darley Richard, Krystle, Scoty and Barry Caddell; great-grandchildren, Caitlyn Allen and Hunter Dyson; a sister, Sylvia Griggs; two brothers, Tony and Bill Richard; and several nieces and nephews. The family will receive friends Thursday from 6 to 8 p.m. at Smith & Williams Funeral Home, Kempsville Chapel. A funeral Mass will be held at 11 a.m. Friday at Ascension Catholic Church by Father James E. Parke.

Interment with full military honors will be held at 3 p.m. Nov. 15, 2005, at Arlington National Cemetery.

In lieu of flowers, memorial donations may be given in his name to the Children’s Hospital of The King’s Daughters, St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital or Operation Smile. Friends may visit his memorial at www.mem.com.  [http://www.mem.com/display/biography.asp?ID=993151]
